Part 1 — Twelve questions

Answer what's easy. Even rough notes help us arrive up to speed.

Walk us through a single client's year-end as it works today — from the files you receive to the statements and returns you file. Where does the time go?
Roughly how many client entities do you act for, and what's the split across companies, sole traders, partnerships/LTCs, and trusts?
For a typical client, what form does the data arrive in — raw bank/CSV exports, a coded trial balance, a shoebox of spreadsheets, a Xero/MYOB file, or a mix?
What do you use for workpapers, financial statements, and tax returns (Xero, MYOB, Workpapers, APS, CCH, Thomson Reuters, Excel)? What stays, what hurts?
Do clients report under special-purpose (SPFR) statements, or do some need general-purpose/GAAP? Any special rules (farming, property, look-through)?
Do you run a standard chart of accounts across clients, or is it per-client? (You can share a template via the secure link below.)
What's the spread of GST setups (registered/not, payments/invoice/hybrid, frequency)? Do you prepare payroll/payday/FBT figures, or consume payroll-software reports?
Are you a registered tax agent with clients linked in myIR (extension of time)? This affects due dates and how returns are lodged.
Who prepares vs. who reviews? What does your sign-off / quality-control step look like, and what would you want the tool to flag for review?
What final deliverables do clients get (PDF statements, a letter, the return), and what branding/template should the tool's output match?
Any requirements on where client data is stored (NZ residency), retention, and access control across your team?
What would make this a clear win at 3 and 12 months (hours saved per job? jobs per preparer?), what's your timeline, and is there a budget you're working within?
